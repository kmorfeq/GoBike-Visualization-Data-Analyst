Preliminary Wrangling¶

This document explores a dataset containing duration_sec and attributes for approximately 519700 bike trip starting from 28th of July 2017 until the end of 2017.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

Trip's duration and number of trips will be the main features.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

To investigate trip's duration and count I'll study the following features: user_type, subscriber_birth_year, Day of week and subscriber_gender.

Duration(seconds) in the dataset take on a very large range of values, from about 61 seconds at the lowest, to about 86,369 seconds at the highest. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of Trip duration takes on a multimodal shape.

The plot suggested that the Day_of_Week has no effect on trip's duration distributions.

def log_trans(x, inverse = False):
    """ quick function for computing log and power operations """
    if not inverse:
        return np.log10(x)
    else:
        return np.power(10, x)

bike['log_duration_min'] = bike['duration_min'].apply(log_trans)

fig, ax = plt.subplots(ncols = 1, nrows = 1 , figsize=[14.70, 8.27])
default_color = sb.color_palette()[0]

sb.violinplot(data = bike, x = 'Day_of_Week', y = 'log_duration_min', ax = ax, color = default_color)
ax.set_yticks(log_trans(np.array([10, 100, 1000])))
ax.set_yticklabels([10, 100, '1k'])
plt.title("Trip Duration by Day of Week")
plt.xlabel('Day of Week')
plt.ylabel('Trip Duration (min)')
plt.show()
